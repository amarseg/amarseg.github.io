Summary

Currently I am a researcher at the London Institute of Medical Sciences. Although I am a molecular biologist by training, I have been interpreting and visualizing the genome-wide datasets I produce experimentally using R since I started my PhD. My research involves the use of statistics to interpret complex biological datasets to produce testable hypotheses, in close collaboration with other research groups. I am interested in applying and developing my programming and data analysis skills.

Omics analysis

  • Experience generating NGS data using Illumina protocols and mass-spectrometry proteomics
  • Use of next generation sequencing quality control (FastQC) and visualisation tools (igv)
  • Familiar with alignment tools (TopHat2, Bowtie2) and other software necessary for the processing of raw reads (samtools, bedtools, HTseq)
  • Knowledge of biological databases (UCSC, Ensembl, KEGG, GEO), the Bioconductor package ecosystem and Ingenuity Pathway Analysis software (IPA)
  • Understanding of the computational pipeline to analyse mass-spectrometry data (Mascot, Progenesis)

Data analysis

  • Identified transcripts involved in the activation of stem cells in the mouse brain using R (DESeq2), combined with the output from Ingenuity Pathway Analysis
  • Used R (ggplot2) to produce rich visualisations (like these ones) of omics datasets
  • Determined transcripts and proteins correlated with growth rate when fission yeast is exposed to different environments
  • Able to mine different kinds of biological data (ChIP-seq, RNA-seq, proteomics) and integrate them in order to explore new hypotheses or support conclusions from other analyses

Programming

  • Used R to infer differentially expressed transcripts and proteins from time-course proteomics and transcriptomics dataset representing the majority of molecules inside fission yeast cells
  • Parsed text files from a Matlab software (FISH-quant) using Python to create clean datasets that could be visualised easily
  • Knowledge of Linux and HPC environments, where I implemented a pipeline in shell that integrates all tools to process raw next generation sequencing data into a format amenable for statistical analysis
  • Use of version control software (Git) together with an R IDE (R Studio)

Communication

  • Carried out productive collaborations with groups in other fields such as mathematics and cell biology
  • Presented my research at two international conferences (qBio in Nashville, and the Pombe Meeting in Barcelona) with more than 200 attendees
  • Gave talks about synthetic biology at two high schools in my home town as part of an initiative to increase awareness of the subject
  • Supervised 3 MSc. and 2 BSc. students during their stay in the laboratory, helping them manage their own projects and teaching how to carry out experiments
