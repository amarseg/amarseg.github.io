Summary

Currently I am a researcher at the London Institute of Medical Sciences. Although I am a molecular biologist by training, I have been interpreting and visualizing the genome-wide datasets I produce experimentally using R since I started my PhD. My research involves the use of statistics to interpret complex biological datasets to produce testable hypotheses, in close collaboration with other research groups. I am interested in applying and developing my programming and data analysis skills.

Omics analysis

  • Experience generating NGS data using Illumina protocols and mass-spectrometry proteomics
  • Use of next generation sequencing quality control (FastQC) and visualisation tools (igv)
  • Familiar with alignment tools (TopHat2, Bowtie2) and other software necessary for the processing of raw reads (samtools, bedtools, HTseq)
  • Knowledge of biological databases (UCSC, Ensembl, KEGG, GEO), the Bioconductor package ecosystem and Ingenuity Pathway Analysis software (IPA)
  • Understanding of the computational pipeline to analyse mass-spectrometry data (Mascot, Progenesis)
